// About DeviceWatch

The national stolen device registry

DeviceWatch is a free, open platform that allows anyone in Malawi to check whether a device has been reported stolen before purchasing it — and allows theft victims to register their stolen devices for national visibility.

Why this exists

Device theft is a growing problem across Malawi and Southern Africa. Stolen phones, laptops, and tablets enter the second-hand market within hours, and buyers have no way to verify whether a device is clean or stolen. DeviceWatch closes that gap.

By creating a publicly searchable registry backed by verified police reports and National ID authentication, we make it risky to sell stolen devices and easy for buyers to protect themselves.

How verification works

IDENTITY
🪪

National ID Check

Every registrant must provide a valid National ID number. In production, this is verified against the national population registry API to prevent fraudulent reports.

POLICE
📋

Police Report Validation

A valid police report number in the format MW/XX/YYYY/NNNNN is required. In production, this is cross-referenced with the Malawi Police Service database.

DEVICE
📱

IMEI Verification

IMEI numbers are validated using the Luhn algorithm (real, implemented). This ensures only legitimate IMEI numbers enter the registry.

PRIVACY
🔒

Owner Protection

Personal details of the device owner are never shown in search results. Only device description, report number, and identifying marks are public.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it free?

Yes. Checking and registering devices is free. The platform is sustained through law enforcement partnerships, government grants, and optional premium API access for businesses.

Do I need an account to check?

No. Anyone can search the registry without creating an account. You only need an account to register a stolen device.

What if I find my device?

Log in to your dashboard and mark the device as "recovered". The public status updates immediately and the device is no longer flagged as stolen.

Can sellers use this?

Yes. Second-hand device sellers can check every device before purchasing from suppliers. This protects their business and customers from unknowingly buying stolen goods.

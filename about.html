// About DeviceWatch

Protecting devices.
Nationally.

DeviceWatch is a free national stolen device registry that connects victims, buyers, and law enforcement through a single verified database — making the second-hand device market safer for everyone.

The Problem

Device theft is rampant. Stolen smartphones and laptops are resold within hours through informal markets and online listings. Buyers have no way to verify if a device is legitimately owned. Victims have no central place to register their stolen device.

Police reports get filed, devices get stolen, and stolen goods circulate openly because there's no connected, searchable registry that buyers at the point-of-sale can access instantly.

The Solution

DeviceWatch is a verified registry. When your device is stolen, you register it with your verified National ID and police report number. The device's IMEI or serial number is indexed in the national registry.

Any buyer — at a market stall, online, or anywhere — can type the IMEI into DeviceWatch and immediately know: is this device stolen?

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it free to use?
Yes. Checking a device is always free. Registering a stolen device is free. We sustain the platform through API partnerships with insurers and law enforcement.
What's shown if a device is found stolen?
The device make/model, IMEI, police report number, theft date, and identifying marks. Your personal details (name, National ID) are never shown publicly.
Why do you need my National ID?
To prevent false reports and abuse. We verify your identity against the national registry — but we never display your ID publicly. It's stored encrypted.
What if I recover my device?
You can log in to your account and update the status to Recovered, which removes the stolen flag from search results immediately.
Can police access the database?
Law enforcement partners can request access through a formal API agreement, allowing them to cross-reference recovered devices against reports.
Is this only for Kenya?
The demo is built with a Kenyan context in mind but the system is designed to be country-agnostic. National ID API integration varies by country.

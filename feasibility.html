Technical & commercial feasibility

An analysis of the technical, legal, and commercial viability of a national stolen device registry for Malawi.

1. Problem Statement

Device theft in Malawi results in significant financial loss for individuals and creates a thriving market for stolen goods. No centralized, publicly accessible registry exists for buyers to verify device provenance. Police reports are filed but not digitally connected to any consumer-facing lookup system.

The consequence is that stolen devices are resold within hours, often at informal markets, with no traceability. Victims have no recourse beyond the initial police report, and buyers unknowingly purchase stolen property.

2. Proposed Solution

A web-based platform where theft victims register stolen devices using verified identities and validated police reports. The registry is publicly searchable by IMEI or serial number, allowing prospective buyers to check devices before purchase.

🌐

Public IMEI Lookup

Free, instant, no account required. Works on any phone browser.

🪪

Verified Registration

National ID and police report validation prevent false reports.

📡

API Access

REST API for integration with police systems, mobile operators, and marketplaces.

4. Integration Partners

Malawi Police Service

Police report verification API. Validates report numbers against the national crime database in real-time.

National Registration Bureau

National ID verification API. Confirms identity of registrants to prevent fraudulent device reports.

MACRA

Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority. Potential IMEI blacklisting integration for network-level blocking of stolen devices.

Mobile Operators

Airtel, TNM. Potential integration for IMEI blocking on cellular networks when a device is flagged as stolen.
